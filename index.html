Amy Del Bosco – Your future Account Integrity Specialist

A Reverse Application

This quirky and type A/ISTJ individual is looking join a creative goal orientated team that offers challenging yet fulfilling assignments in an environment that inspires greatness. This would involve utilizing her current knowledge and skill-set while providing an opportunity to stretch herself in meaningful work that makes a real impact.

While she can get along with almost everyone, she understands just how important it is that the company's culture aligns with her own beliefs and personality. The end goal is finding the perfect position that keeps her looking forward to coming into work every day and giving her all.

What to expect from her:

  • Perfectionism and being highly organized - Her significant other jokes that her organization system has an organization system (it's true, ask him)
  • Going beyond - rarely satisfied with just doing 'good enough' in order to check off the box. Not afraid to get her hands dirty and doing extra work to get the job done
  • Acting ethically - no seriously, she majored in Ethics as part of her undergrad so don't ask her to do anything questionable or she'll start discussing the Ford Pinto cover-up
  • Resourcefulness - she has become one with Google search and has zero apprehension asking others for help
  • Awesome communication skills - feels at ease talking to all levels of management. Has a healthy respect for positions they have earned but believes in treating everyone the same
  • Honesty - never afraid to admit that she has made a mistake or doesn't have the answer, but won't stop until she makes it right or finds the solution
  • Life long learner - enjoys learning new and creative solutions in order to solve problems
  • Open minded - she believes change is inevitable, and is always willing to entertain ideas especially if it will increase efficiency or results in getting the competitive edge (and is ethical, see Acting ethically)
  • Cautiously Adventurous - not afraid to try new things at least once but only after it has been well thought out, unless it involves climbing or heights (ya, that's a nope, nope, nope for her)
  • Keeps getting up after being knocked down ... to a point. She's human but always comes out learning something from the experience
  • Thrives on chaos and secretly hopes things break - her love of risk mitigation and response planning lead her to get a Master of Disaster and Emergency Management. Yes, it is a real thing and no, she doesn't bury gold in the backyard in preparation for the apocalypse. Besides, everyone knows toilet paper will be the new currency

What type of work environment motivates her:

  • Working collaboratively with creative and talented people towards a common goal, where people strive to produce quality work and have fun while doing it
  • Team members are encouraged to be themselves, share ideas and are allowed the freedom to take the initiative to get the job done
  • Stimulating projects, drawing upon and fully utilizing each team member’s strengths to provide meaning and purpose within their role within on the team
  • Open door policies, that fosters transparent and open communication and builds trust
  • The ability to join other team’s projects, or the possibility of rotational assignments
  • Hard work is duly recognized and appropriately rewarded in a meaningful manner
  • Opportunities for professional and personal development
  • Explicit expectations with clearly direction; work performance reviews occurs more frequently than once a year
  • Less formal atmosphere that values substance over appearance
  • Focus is on long-term success and sustainability rather than band-aid solutions and short term gains
