Amy Del Bosco – A few interesting facts


FAVORITE COLOUR?

Purple, hands down. Purple is royal and strong, but at the same time gentle and soothing. Plus, purple looks great with yellow and green.

CATS OR DOGS?

Cats! Don’t get me wrong; dogs are great loyal companions, but cats are intelligent, independent and have no issues being themselves. If money was no object, I would run a cat cafe or a boarding house for stray cats. One of my most treasured memories was when my pregnant foster cat ‘collected’ me to be at her side while she brought four little fur nuggets into this world. It is hard to explain the honour and love one feels by being chosen by an animal.

EARLY BIRD OR NIGHT OWL?

I do not consider myself a morning person. But, this is why I prefer to come into work at 7:30 in the morning, so that I can collect voice messages, read new e-mail, and deal with the mundane items. This way, when the rest of the team gets into the office my brain is free of clutter and ready to get down to business.

FAVOURITE CARTOONS?

Archer, Venture Brothers, Rick & Morty, Morel Orel. If I have to explain their awesomeness, I don’t think we can be friends.

FAVOURITE YOUTUBE CHANNELS? AND WHY?

The School Of Life, as their videos help to answer many of the deeper questions, like why we are the way we are, and does so in an attention-grabbing and thought-provoking way. Philip DeFranco, since he talks about news and pop culture in a humorous and captivating fashion. Gary Vaynerchuk, as I find his videos extremely motivating and appreciate his no-bs attitude. And LastWeekTonight, primarily due to the fact that John Oliver deep dives into current events and present the news and politics is a provocative and stimulating manner.

BEST VACATION?

This is a difficult toss-up between a day-trip to see polar bears in Churchill, MB and spending a week exploring Iceland. I like to vacation in colder climates, and how cool is it to say I’ve seen polar bears in their natural environment? Or that, in Iceland I was able to see the northern lights on a glacier, walk behind a waterfall, walk barefoot in volcanic ash, and experience horse meat all in the same day in one of the most friendliest and breathtaking places on earth.

FAVOURITE BOARD GAME?

Pandemic. Not only is the topic of the game in line with what I studied in my Masters, but it is also a collaborative game that enables players to work in partnership against the board. Even though it is only a game, there is a sense of accomplishment from winning as a team.

WHAT IS YOUR FAVORITE DESSERT?

Chocolate. Anything chocolate. Just make sure I'm wearing a bib, because I have a tendency to get it everywhere like a little kid.

WHAT MUSIC DO YOU LISTEN TO ON THE BUS?

Actually, I don’t listen to music while on the bus. I am more likely to strike up a conversation with my seatmate or people watch. I find that music distracts from the full experience.

WHAT TEACHER INFLUENCED YOU THE MOST?

Lieutenant Colonel Walter Perchal, who taught Terrorism in the Global Context, Terrorism in Canadian Context, Applied Topics in Business Continuity at York University. The greatest lesson I learned from him was the wisdom in answering ‘it depends’; the answer is never a simple black and white solution in the majority cases.
